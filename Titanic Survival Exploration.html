ABSTRACT

The sinking of the RMS Titanic is one of the most infamous shipwrecks in history. On April 15, 1912, during her maiden voyage, the Titanic sank after colliding with an iceberg, killing 1502 out of 2224 passengers and crew. This sensational tragedy shocked the international community and led to better safety regulations for ships. This project Investigates the titanic's sample dataset consisting of 891 entries using Python, Numpy and Pandas for data wrangling and analysis and using matplotlib.pyplot library along with seaborn for data visualization in Ipython Notebook.



SUMMARY


RMS Titanic was a British passenger liner that sank in the North Atlantic Ocean in the early morning of 15 April 1912, after colliding with an iceberg during her maiden voyage from Southampton to New York City. Of the 2,224 passengers and crew aboard, more than 1,500 died in the sinking, making it one of the deadliest commercial peacetime maritime disasters in modern history. The largest ship afloat at the time it entered service, the RMS Titanic was the second of three Olympic class ocean liners operated by the White Star Line, and was built by the Harland and Wolff shipyard in Belfast. Thomas Andrews, her architect, died in the disaster.

Under the command of Edward Smith, the ship carried some of the wealthiest people in the world, as well as hundreds of emigrants from Great Britain and Ireland, Scandinavia and elsewhere throughout Europe seeking a new life in North America. A high-power radiotelegraph transmitter was available for sending passenger "marconigrams" and for the ship's operational use. Although Titanic had advanced safety features such as watertight compartments and remotely activated watertight doors, there were not enough lifeboats to accommodate all of those aboard due to outdated maritime safety regulations. Titanic only carried enough lifeboats for 1,178 people—slightly more than half of the number on board, and one third of her total capacity.

This project Investigates the titanic's sample dataset consisting of 891 entries using Python programming language, Numpy and Pandas for data wrangling and data analysis and uses matplotlib library along with seaborn for data visualisation in Ipython Notebook. The scope of this project is to dive deeper into the data of the passenger, whether they survived or not, were males or females, minors or adults, their embarked location and passenger class etc. to identify what affected their chances of survival and how the variable were affected in relation to one another.

By the common law the age of majority is fixed at twenty-one years for both sexes, and, in the absence of any statute to the contrary, every person under that age, whether male or female, is an infant.

-- English Common Law

Now we will split the Age data into Minors (less than 21 years) and Adults (Greater than 21 years) to determine how many passengers were adults and how many were minors and if their survivability could be affected in this case as children had priority to lifeboats that could increase their chances of survival.

A function "minor_or_adult" is defined that will pull the age and sex data from our titanic_data and if the age is less than 21 then it will declare "minor" else it will declare "adult" under a separate column created in our dataframe by the name "status". This will help us in distinguishing the minors from the adults in a separate column and not overrite our existing dataframe.

In [28]:
#Splitting the Age Data in 3 categories (child, adult male and adult female)
def minor_or_adult(passengers):
    age, sex = passengers
    
    if age < 21:
        return "minor"
    else:
        return sex
    
    #including new column to a data frame that distinguishes a passenger as a child or an adult
titanic_data['status'] = titanic_data[['Age', 'Sex']].apply(minor_or_adult, axis=1)

CONCLUSION



Gender was an important factor that affected the survivability of the passengers significantly. During emergencies, women and children were given a priority and considering this factor, during the sinking of the ship, women and children were given priority for lifeboats than men. Age was a factor too that affected the survivability but not as important as gender. Age played an important role for minor passengers under the age of 13 - 15 years. However, from 15 years, age didnt increased or decresed the chances of survival of the passengers and that was one of the reasons why the chances of survival of female adults was more than the survival of the minors.

The passenger class also played a very important role in determining the survivability of the passengers. The maximum number of passengers were in Pclass 3 which was the poorest class followed by Pclass 1 which was considered a rich class. The chances of survival for passengers in Pclass 1 were much higher than the chances of survival of passengers in Pclass 2 or 3. This showed that the Rich passengers were given a priority for lifeboats than the poor passengers on board.

Many passengers were on board without a family, maximum being the males between the age of 18 to 35 years who stayed in Pclass 3. The probability of survival of passengers with family was about 50% whereas the probability of survival of passengers without a family was about 30%. The people who were accompanied by their parents or siblings, did had a higher chance of survival.




LIMITATIONS OF THE ANALYSIS



1) The data analysis and results are based on the sample data consisting of 891 entries where as the total number of passengers on board were 2224 so the actual results can vary.

2) The data is not fully complete. Columns like Age, Cabin and Embarked had NaN values which can make the actual results differ from the results generated.

3) Other family relatives were excluded from this study include cousins,nephews/nieces, aunts/uncles, and in-laws. Some children travelled only with a nanny, therefore parch=0 for them. As well, some travelled with very close friends or neighbors in a village, however, this thing was not taken into account.
